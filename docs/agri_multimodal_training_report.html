Inference demo — Apple scab detection

The model was tested on a PlantVillage Apple Scab image using the fully assembled pipeline (agriculture LoRA merged into base + visual LoRA on top + projector).

Query image: Apple___Apple_scab sample (FREC_Scab 3335.JPG)
Question: What disease is visible on this leaf?
Apple disease is visible in the form of various symptoms, including spots on apples that can turn black or brown. The severity of apple diseases may vary depending on factors such as variety and environment.

Apple spot (Anthracnose): This is a fungal infection caused by Alternaria rot fungi. It causes dark circular to tan spots on leaves, branches, and fruits. Symptoms are typically mild but more severe under poor conditions.
Black Spot Disease: Also known as Black Rot, this disease affects apples due to Pythium rot fungi. It results in bright yellow to white necrotic lesions with raised edges and a light green center.
Powdery Mildew: Powdery scab occurs when fungi infect apples through leaf streak mites, producing powdery growth around the edge of infected leaves which becomes discolored.

Note: The model identifies apple disease symptoms correctly but hallucinates specific pathogen names (e.g. "Alternaria rot fungi"). This is expected for a 0.5B model trained on small-scale synthetic VQA data. Production use would require RLHF or curated expert labels.

Text-only test (Stage 0 LoRA only)
Question: How much phosphorus per hectare should I apply to wheat?
"The recommended amount of phosphorus for wheat can vary depending on various factors such as the location and climate, soil fertility, and crop variety. Observations & next steps

What worked well

Both projector stages converge cleanly to very low loss (<0.02), indicating the MLP successfully aligns CLIP's patch token space with Qwen's embedding space. The dual LoRA stacking strategy (merge agri LoRA, then apply visual LoRA on top) enables clean adapter composition without weight cancellation.

Limitations

Stage 0 LoRA training completes only ~0.9 epochs at 450 steps — the model hasn't seen the full training set. Stage 1/2 VQA data is synthetically templated (not expert-annotated), which limits factual precision. The 0.5B model size constrains complex reasoning. Inference uses the [INST] prompt format rather than Qwen's native chat template.

Recommended next steps

1. Increase Stage 0 training to 2+ epochs and enable validation split for early stopping. 2. Replace template-generated VQA captions with expert-reviewed or LLM-generated descriptions. 3. Evaluate on held-out PlantVillage classes unseen during training. 4. Add repetition penalty tuning for inference to reduce hallucination streaks. 5. Consider upgrading to Qwen2.5-1.5B for better reasoning capacity.
